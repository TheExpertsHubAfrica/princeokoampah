About me

Prince Okoampah is a distinguished voice in Africa’s emerging leadership and digital transformation space. As CEO of TET Africa and The Experts Hub Africa, he brings a rare blend of strategic insight, technological innovation and human-centred branding that empowers rising leaders to build meaningful impact at scale.

Honoured with the Global Excellence Business Leader Award by the HonorCrest Institute (Canada), Porto Business School (Portugal) and M-Gibes College of Business & Management (UK & Ghana), Prince’s influence spans across boardrooms and grassroots platforms. His recognition at a landmark event in Johannesburg, South Africa underscores a reputation built not just on results but on substance and vision.

Equipped with an MBA in Leadership for Multilateral Organisations from Atlanta College of Liberal Arts and Sciences (Georgia, USA), Prince fuses deep strategic thinking with psychological intelligence to help CEOs under 40 build authentic brands, integrate Technology & AI into their leadership practices and drive faster, smarter decision-making.

As the founder of The EmpowerED Talks and Trail Blazers Africa, he curates conversations that cut through noise—reaching thousands with practical frameworks for purpose-driven leadership, personal clarity and exponential growth. He has trained TEDx speakers, coached C-suite executives and delivered keynotes that leave lasting, measurable impact.

But Prince’s story didn’t begin on grand stages. Raised in a humble home in Accra, Ghana, his first public speaking engagement drew just 12 people. Years later, he would speak with up to 50,000 audiences present. It was during one such event in 2018 and 2021 at Ota, Lagos - Nigeria that he received international recognition for a compelling portrayal of Bishop David Oyedepo—earning him the cognomen “Prince Okoh Afa.”

Beyond accolades, Prince is revered for his ability to “read people” intuitively and help them unlock their personal “superpowers” to achieve relevant results and come up with meaningful solutions to problems. He is not a speaker who merely talks—he listens, translates complexity into clarity and moves people into action.

His ethos is simple yet profound:
“Be yourself. It’s not that hard.”

For organizations seeking a speaker who can connect deeply, challenge perspectives and equip audiences with transformative tools—they’ll find more than a speaker in Prince Okoampah. They’ll find a partner in progress.
